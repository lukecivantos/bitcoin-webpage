What is Bitcoin?


Bitcoin is a digital currency, decentralized from any governing body, that allows user to user transfers without the need for a middleman.This is possible through the ingenious invention of the blockchain, software that relies on verifications from multiple parties to make any transaction valid. For verifying these transactions, the parties, known as miners, are rewarded with Bitcoins of their own.

Technology Behind Bitcoin



What is blockchain?


A blockchain can be thought of as a decentralized database. This database maintains a growing list of records, or blocks, which are linked in chronological order to the previous block. Bitcoin’s blockchain is a public ledger that contains a record of every previous bitcoin transaction, but provides anonymity to these transactions through preventing the public from seeing the specific details of each transaction.


What is mining?


Transactions using bitcoin are made possible by the blockchain, software that relies on verifications from multiple parties to make any transaction valid. For verifying these transactions, the parties, known as miners, are rewarded with bitcoins of their own. In order to mine a block, the miner’s must find the answer to a complex mathematical problem. This is done by a method of brute force, with miners computers testing millions of different solutions until they find the correct one. Over time, verifying these transactions becomes harder, meaning the average number of attempts to find the solution increases, and more powerful and expensive computers are needed.

Bitcoin Forks


When a contentious issue (such as the maximum block size capacity) cannot be agreed on by bitcoin developers, a fork in bitcoin’s blockchain can occur. During a fork, the bitcoin blockchain diverges into two potential new chains. After a fork both new blockchains will share their previous transaction history, although moving forward all new transactions will only appear in the blockchain they are verified on. Bitcoin Core


January 3rd 2009


Bitcoin Core is the original bitcoin that was published by Satoshi Nakamoto. It was later renamed Bitcoin Core to help distinguish it from other versions of bitcoin created by forks.

Proliferation of Cryptocurrency


As Bitcoin has gained more and more publicity and mainstream appeal, hundreds of alternative cryptocurrencies, known commonly as alt coins, have developed. While many of these alt coins are somewhat obscure, a small number have grown to the point of possessing a significant share of the entire cryptocurrency market. These include Ethereum, Litecoin and recently Bitcoin Cash. Looking Forward


Currently, the majority of bitcoin transitions occur over bitcoin exchanges rather than in a retail capacity. This must change for bitcoin to became a mainstream currency in the future. In order for Bitcoin to become widely accepted as a medium of exchange, it both needs to shed it’s association with the dark web, which still lingers from the high publicity surrounding the silk road website and be supported by more commercial businesses as a means of payment.


The recent news concerning the creation of a bitcoin futures market by CME group Inc. is a huge step in the direction of widespread adoption for bitcoin, giving the cryptocurrency an element of authenticity that is has been lacking thus far. It will be extremely interesting to see how the futures market affects bitcoin’s price volatility, and whether its creation will pave the way for more widespread retail adoption of bitcoin as an acceptable payment method in the future.
